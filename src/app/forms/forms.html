Angular Forms: Template-driven vs Reactive

Template-driven Forms

  • Think of these as “HTML-first” forms.
  • You design the form mostly in the template (HTML), and Angular automatically wires up the logic behind the scenes.
  • They feel easy and quick for small forms (like a login form with just email + password).
  • The template carries most of the responsibility, so your TypeScript file stays light.

Reactive Forms

  • These are “TypeScript-first” forms.
  • You build the form structure in your TypeScript file, then connect it to the template.
  • They give you more control: you can track changes, validate in detail, and react to user input in real time.
  • Perfect for complex forms (like a multi-step checkout with dynamic rules).

Key Differences

Aspect Template-driven Reactive
Approach HTML-first TypeScript-first
Form Setup Defined in template Defined in component class
Control Limited Full control
Validation Basic, template-based Advanced, programmatic
Best for Simple forms Complex forms

Scenario Example

Imagine you’re building a shopping site:

  • Login form (2 fields: email + password) → Use template-driven. It’s quick, simple, and doesn’t need heavy logic.
  • Checkout form (address, payment, coupon, dynamic shipping options, live validation) → Use reactive. You need fine-grained control, dynamic updates, and complex validation.

Summary

  • Template-driven = quick & simple
  • Reactive = powerful & complex
